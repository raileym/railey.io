Combinatorics - Summary

Last updated on Feb 27, 2021 4 min read

The following list of combinatoric identities is a running collection of those identities that I have used to date on my site. The summary is meant to be a quick reference list only without exposition.

Theorem 1 (The Binomial Coefficient) For non-negative \(n\) and \(0 \le k \le n\),

\[\begin{equation} {n \choose k} = \frac {n!}{k!(n-k)!} \end{equation}\]

is called a binomial coefficient.

Theorem 2 (Binomial Coefficient Identity) Let \(n\) and \(k\) be positive integers, where \(k \le n\). Then

\[\begin{equation} {n \choose k} = {n \choose n-k} \end{equation}\]

Proof

\[\begin{aligned} {n \choose k} = & \ \ \frac {n!}{k!(n-k)!} \\ = & \ \ \frac {n!}{(n-k)!k!} \\ = & \ \ \frac {n!}{(n-k)!(n-(n-k))!} \\[1em] = & \ \ {n \choose n-k} \\[1em] & & \rule{0.5em}{0.5em} \end{aligned}\]

When noted in Pascal’s Triangle, these two combinations are symmetric about the Triangle’s center line for given values of \(n\) and \(k\) . Theorem 3 (The Binomial Theorem) The binomial theorem states for real or complex \(a, b,\) and non-negative \(n\),

\[\begin{equation} (a+b)^n = \sum^n_{k=0} {n \choose k} a^{n-k}b^k \end{equation}\]

where \({n \choose k} = \frac {n!}{k!(n-k)!}\) is a binomial coefficient.

Theorem 4 (Pascal’s Identity) Let \(n\) and \(k\) be positive integers, where \(k \le n\). Then

\[\begin{equation} { n \choose k} = {n-1 \choose k-1} + {n-1 \choose k} \end{equation}\]
