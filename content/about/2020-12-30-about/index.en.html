This website is a running commentary on my study and practice of a new discipline called data science. I call this discipline applied statistics when speaking to my friends and family.

Spring 2021 – Let the games begin! I decided over a year ago to study data science formally through an academic program. To that end, I applied to and was accepted to the Master of Science in Data Science Online at the University of Texas at Austin.

Need for change. For some time, I have been writing software to help kids learn math. After so many years to that end, I decided I needed a venue change: I like software engineering challenges but need a different path. I need new math and computer science challenges, including working with new programming languages, new tools, and new frameworks. For more on why I am affecting a change, see my post on why I want to be a data scientist.

Getting a degree. While studying data science is not a stretch for me, doing so for a formal degree is: I already have a Ph.D. in Computer Science plus years of experience in software engineering. Learning a new topic is certainly not a surprise choice on my part. I have always been a life-long learner, studying new topics in technology, mathematics, and taxation. I buy used math books through Amazon, subscribe annually to O’Reilly Learning Platform, and most recently signed up for Coursera Plus. Nonetheless, I decided to take the plunge. To learn more, please see my post on Why I chose to pursue a degree in data science.

Attending UT Austin. Many universities offer graduate programs in data science online. So then the question is where should I attend school. I am motivated to attend UT Austin for several reasons, including UT’s commitment to an in-depth mathematical approach to applied statistics, not to mention my hometown attachment to UT Austin. To learn more about my decision, please see my post on Why I decided to attend the University of Texas at Austin.
