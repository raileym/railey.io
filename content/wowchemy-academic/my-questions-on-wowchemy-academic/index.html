\[\begin{align} N[A] = & k+r-1 \\\\[0.5em] = & k + (r-1) \\\\ \end{align}\]



\[\begin{aligned} N[A] = & k+r-1 \\\\[0.5em] = & k + (r-1) \\\\ \end{aligned}\]

Also, Bookdown says you can add another expression in your latex equation, as in

\[\begin{equation} f\left(k\right) = \binom{n}{k} p^k\left(1-p\right)^{n-k} \tag{1} \end{equation}\]

By using this form, you can then make a reference back to the equation like this Equation (1).
