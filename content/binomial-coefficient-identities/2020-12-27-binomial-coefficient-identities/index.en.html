This post summaries the Binomial Coefficient Identities that appear throughout data science and applied statistics. These identities are used regularly for manipulating expressions for deriving means and variations of certain distributions. r \(\renewcommand{\qedsymbolred} {\color{red} \rule{0.5em}{0.5em}}\) \(\renewcommand{\qedsymbolblack}{\color{black} \rule{0.5em}{0.5em}}\) \(\renewcommand{\qedsymbolwhite}{\color{white} \rule{0.5em}{0.5em}}\) \(\renewcommand{\qedsymbol} {\color{black} \rule{0.5em}{0.5em}}\)

Number one

When deriving the expected value of a random variable with a hypergeometric distribution, we use the following identity in the process,

\[\begin{equation} {N \choose n} = \left( \frac Nn \right) {N-1 \choose n-1}. \end{equation}\]

Proof:

\[\begin{align} {N \choose n} = \ & {N \choose n} \\\\ = \ & \frac{N!} {n! (N-n)!} \\\\ = \ & \frac{N\cdot(N-1)!} {n\cdot(n-1)! (N-n)!} \\\\ = \ & \left( \frac Nn \right) \frac{(N-1)!} {(n-1)! (N-n)!} \\\\ = \ & \left( \frac Nn \right) {N-1 \choose n-1} \ \ \ \ \qedsymbol \end{align}\]

For example (Wackerly, Mendenhall, and Scheaffer 2002, 65),

\[\begin{align} \mathbb{E}[X] = \ & \sum_{x \in S} x \frac{{N_1 \choose x}{N_2 \choose {n-x}}}{\color{red} {N \choose n}} \\\\ = \ & \sum_{x \in S} x \frac{N_1 !}{x! (N_1 - x)!} \frac{{N_2 \choose {n-x}}}{\color{red} \left(\frac Nn \right) {N-1 \choose n-1}} \end{align}\]

Reference

Wackerly, D. D., W. Mendenhall, and R. L. Scheaffer. 2002. Mathematical Statistics with Applications. 6th ed. Pacific Grove, CA: Thomson Learning.
